Artist Statement

Life defies binaries, yet we lie conditions and expectations onto bodies based on invariant physicalities. Created as a tool to sustain the sovereignty against minorities, these socially constructed categories of humans leave a pit for those who don't align with the anticipated semblance—my work examines this manufactured nihility within intersectionality and the severance of belonging it inhabits. As a mixed-race immigrant who grew up in South Korea, I’ve experienced the confusing overlap of the Black and Asian diaspora in the East and the West. As I am estranged from both my birthplace, culture, and race I flow in between the realm of omnipresence and an outlier. It’s this fraught kinship towards place, ethnicity, race, and living that my work builds from.
